# Problem archetypes and trap checklists

## Asymptotic distribution

Check:

- pointwise at every threshold versus continuity points;
- tightness and escape of mass;
- full sequence versus subsequence;
- exact centering and normalization;
- random parameter versus fixed typical parameter;
- moment determinacy;
- limit interchange;
- hidden independence assumptions.

## Extremal constant

Check:

- infimum/supremum versus minimum/maximum;
- whether extremizers exist;
- compactness of the admissible class;
- degree or dimension allowed to vary;
- repeated roots or multiplicities;
- strict versus non-strict sublevel sets;
- boundary roots and degenerate objects;
- scaling invariance.

## Density and additive combinatorics

Check:

- upper, lower, logarithmic, Banach, and natural density distinctions;
- translation invariance;
- finite versus infinite sets;
- zero-density exceptional sets;
- dependence of constants on forbidden configurations;
- local-to-global compatibility;
- pseudorandomness assumptions.

## Graph covering or decomposition

Check:

- loops and parallel edges;
- disconnected graphs;
- bridges and cutvertices;
- exact multiplicity;
- trails masquerading as cycles;
- induced versus non-induced structures;
- reductions that introduce forbidden edges;
- cubic or connectivity assumptions not present in the statement.

## Polynomial or analytic inequality

Check:

- monicity and degree;
- root multiplicity;
- real versus complex roots;
- endpoint roots;
- measure of strict sublevel sets;
- logarithm branches;
- singular values;
- equality and limiting cases;
- use of compactness across varying degree.

## Recursive or Diophantine sequence

Check:

- initial conditions;
- integer versus real domain;
- exceptional small indices;
- exact divisibility and valuation;
- uniformity in parameters;
- rational approximation regimes;
- periodic or near-periodic counterexamples;
- empirical growth mistaken for asymptotics.
